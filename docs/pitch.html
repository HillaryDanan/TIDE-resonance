What I Do

I identify and document unexpected behaviors in AI systems through systematic testing methodologies derived from neurodiversity research. My approach reveals processing patterns that standard testing often misses.

Unique Methodology

My neuroscience PhD research discovered that different human brains achieve identical outcomes through alternative neural pathways. I apply these insights to AI systems, developing tools that detect when models use non-standard processing routes to reach conclusions.

Core Approach

  • Pattern Detection: Identifying alternative processing pathways in AI systems
  • Behavioral Analysis: Documenting how different architectures handle identical tasks
  • Edge Case Discovery: Finding system behaviors outside normal parameters
  • Empirical Testing: Building tools to measure and visualize these phenomena

Background

PhD in Cognitive Neuroscience - Studied neural representation of abstract concepts in neurotypical and autistic individuals through 60+ fMRI sessions. Discovered that different cognitive styles use distinct neural pathways to achieve the same conceptual understanding.

Key Finding: "Alternative processing pathways are not deficits but equally valid solutions to cognitive challenges."

This biological insight now drives my AI research: different architectures may solve problems through unexpected pathways, and understanding these alternatives is crucial for AI safety and interpretability.
